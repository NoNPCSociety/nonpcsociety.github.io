# marumaruNFT (MARU): Exchange Listings, Corporate Partnerships, and the Full Record

A factual account of what marumaruNFT built, who verified it, and what the record shows.

---

marumaruNFT is a closed project. The token (MARU) was delisted in December 2025. The founder has written elsewhere about what went wrong and what he learned. This document serves a different purpose: it is a factual record of what the project achieved during its operation, verified by third parties who applied their own due diligence before engaging.

The record matters because characterizations of marumaruNFT as a scam or rug pull continue to circulate. Those characterizations are factually inconsistent with the documented history below.

---

## Market Performance

MARU launched in September 2022. At peak, the token achieved approximately 500x appreciation from presale pricing. Liquidity reached approximately $6,000,000 at its highest point — a level that reflects sustained, organic capital accumulation over time, not a short-term spike.

The price trajectory sustained upward movement for approximately twelve months post-launch, with a second peak that created meaningful profit opportunities for participants who held through it. This is not the profile of a pump-and-dump or fraudulent operation. Scams extract capital quickly and disappear. MARU traded publicly for over three years.

## CoinW Exchange Listing

In approximately April 2024, MARU was listed on CoinW, a centralized cryptocurrency exchange. CEX listings require independent due diligence conducted by the exchange — legal review, team verification, tokenomics assessment, and market viability screening. Projects that do not pass this process are not listed.

Following listing, MARU was adopted into CoinW's asset management floor and selected as a staking target — designations that require additional internal review beyond the base listing process. At peak, MARU held priority token status on the platform.

As trading volume declined through 2025, the token's classification was downgraded to the challenge token floor. It was delisted in December 2025 — consistent with standard exchange policy for tokens with insufficient sustained volume.

A project operated with fraudulent intent does not accumulate a year of CEX operation, staking designation, and priority status before an orderly volume-driven delisting. Rug pulls do not have exchange histories. This one does.

## Tokyo Stock Exchange-Listed Corporate Partnership

In October 2024, THE WHY HOW DO COMPANY, Inc. — listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange Standard Market under securities code 3823 — made two separate timely disclosures through Japan's TDnet regulatory filing system regarding its 100% subsidiary WHDC Acrodea Co., Ltd. and its partnership with Personpower Co., Ltd., the operating company of marumaruNFT.

The first disclosure announced the execution of a strategic business partnership agreement among WHDC Acrodea, Personpower, and AKIBA Tourism Council Co., Ltd.

The second disclosure announced the installation of a marumaruAR experience booth at the Cho Maid Mura in AKIHABARA Cultural Festival 2024 — confirming active operational participation in the partnership, not merely its announcement.

TDnet filings are submitted under Tokyo Stock Exchange rules governing timely disclosure of material corporate information. Listed companies bear legal liability for the accuracy of these filings. Formal due diligence precedes any named external partnership disclosed under these obligations.

## AKIBA Tourism Council

AKIBA Tourism Council Co., Ltd. was established in 2018 by Akihabara business operators as a formal corporate entity, following the district's 2016 designation by Japan's Agency for Tourism as a supported region under the "Tourism Destination Appeal Creation Project." The organization represents a network of over 700 Akihabara-area businesses.

marumaruNFT participated in the 2024 Cho Maid Mura in AKIHABARA Cultural Festival as a sponsor and active participant — an event organized under this institutional framework, and separately confirmed in a TSE timely disclosure filing by a listed company.

Public institutions and tourism organizations operating under government designation do not co-organize events with entities they have reason to believe are fraudulent.
